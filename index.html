Noon Curve Concept

20 years of data · NY Session 8:00 AM – 4:00 PM · High/low formation relative to 12:00 PM

Core Finding
74.3%
The session high and low form on opposite sides of 12:00 PM. One forms in the AM session, the other in the PM session.
Implication: If a clear high or low is established in the AM session, there is strong expectancy (74.3%) that the opposite extreme will form in the PM session. Trade the PM leg.
Quarterly Structure (2-Hour Blocks)
Q1 — 8:00–10:00 AM
Sets the AM high or low. 66% of PM-high days → Q1 set the AM low. 68% of PM-low days → Q1 set the AM high.
Q2 — 10:00–12:00 PM
If Q1 established both high and low, and Q2 breaks one of those levels — this signals the opposite extreme will be taken in PM. High-conviction signal.
Q3 — 12:00–2:00 PM
PM session begins. If AM established a clear extreme, expect PM to form the opposite. Noon is the structural pivot of the day.
Q4 — 2:00–4:00 PM
Closing session. Continuation of PM directional move or reversal toward close. Low statistical edge for new setups.
Noon Curve — Practical Trading Rules
RULE 1

Q1 Establishes the Extreme

If Q1 (8–10 AM) sets a clear high or low that holds into Q2, this becomes your "opposite side" target for the PM session. 66–68% of days, Q1 is where the AM extreme forms.

RULE 2

Q2 Break Confirms PM Direction

If Q2 breaks one of the Q1 extremes (both high and low were set in Q1), treat this as a signal that the opposite Q1 extreme will be taken during the PM session (Q3–Q4).

RULE 3

Trade the PM Leg at 12:00 PM

74.3% of days form their session high and low on opposite sides of noon. After noon, look for the PM directional move toward the opposite session extreme. Combine with IB60 targets for precise entries.

RULE 4

High/Low Wicks — Early/Late Candles

Swings formed in the first 20 minutes of the 9:00 AM hour are less likely to hold (pre-RTH volatility). Early and late segments of candles often form wicks; middle segments contain expansion. Don't trust pre-open spikes as session highs/lows.

Daily Prep Checklist

Pre-session workflow — synthesized from all statistical research

Pre-Session (Before 7AM ET)
6:45

Mark the 2AM London Midpoint

Calculate Mid = (2AM High + 2AM Low) / 2. Note if it was already touched. If untouched, it's an active 93.5% reference target.

6:50

Identify Overnight Pattern

Which of the 4 ALN patterns? London Engulfs Asia / Asia Engulfs London / Partial Up / Partial Down. This sets your structural bias.

6:55

Mark Key Overnight Levels

Asia High, Asia Low, London High, London Low, Midnight Open. Note hit rates for each (67–79%). These are your morning targets.

7:00

Check pRTH Position

Will today's RTH likely open inside or outside prior day's RTH? Outside = 83% chance opposite side holds. Inside = 73% chance at least one side breaks.

7AM–9:30AM ET Window
7:59

Mark 7AM Magic Hour Mid

Mid = (7AM High + 7AM Low) / 2. Is 2AM mid still untouched? If both mids converge within 10 pts → EXTREME CONFLUENCE. Treat as primary target.

8:00

Determine NY Open Position

Is NY opening above or below the London session midpoint? Above → 77.6% hit London high first. Below → 74.1% hit London low first. This is your primary directional filter.

8:00

Primary Midpoint Trading Window

68.7% of 7AM mid touches happen 8:00–8:30 ET. Any untouched 2AM mid also resolves high probability here. Execute fades toward untouched mids in this window.

8:30

Check Data Releases

Strong NFP/CPI prints can override midpoint fades (9.9% of never-touched 7AM mid days). Wait for post-data stabilization before executing.

RTH Session (9:30AM–12:00PM ET)
9:30

IB30 Closes — Note Position

Did IB30 close in upper or lower half of range? Upper → 81% IB high breaks. Lower → 74% IB low breaks. This gives directional bias for IB expansion trade.

10:30

IB60 Closes — Full Analysis

Calculate IB60 range. Calculate IB60 vol% of RTH volume. Apply regression: RTH ≈ (IB60 × 1.48) + 69. Set T1 and T2 extension targets. Assess vol% tier for expansion sizing.

10:30

Wait for First IB Break

68% of breaks occur 10:30–11:59 ET. Once IB breaks in one direction, set targets. On one-directional days, opposite side = 0% close probability — clean asymmetry.

12:00

NOON HARD STOP — IB Trades

Stop all IB expansion setups. The edge window has largely passed. If no IB break by noon, skip — late breaks produce compressed 131pt median range.

PM Session (12:00PM–4:00PM ET)
12:00

Noon Curve Assessment

Was an AM extreme clearly established in Q1 (8–10 AM)? If yes → 74.3% probability the PM session forms the OPPOSITE extreme. Look for the PM directional leg.

12:00

Q2 Break Confirmation

If Q2 broke one of the Q1 extremes, this confirms the PM will likely take the other Q1 extreme. This is the highest-conviction PM setup in the noon curve framework.

2:00

Q4 Transition (2–4PM)

Reduce size. Edge is diminished. Avoid new directional setups unless extremely clean. Focus on managing existing positions and respecting end-of-day extensions.

4:00

End-of-Day Review

75% of days close inside ±1× IB60 band. 87% inside ±1.5×. Did session follow the overnight pattern? Note any deviations for future calibration.

Hard Rules — Never Break These
✓ ALWAYS mark 2AM and 7AM midpoints. These are the two highest-probability intraday reference levels (93.5% and 90.1%). Non-negotiable daily setup.
✓ NY open position above/below London mid is your primary filter. Determine this before any other analysis. 77.6% / 74.1% directional accuracy.
✓ Never abandon a midpoint because price moved far. The paradox is confirmed: return probability INCREASES with distance. 100+ pt excursions from 2AM mid returned 100% of the time.
✓ Partial at T1, runner to T2. Touch-to-close probability is ~55% at both T1 and T2 — treat them symmetrically. Take partial at T1, hold remainder with trailing stop.
✗ STOP trading IB expansion setups at noon. 68% of edge captured before noon. Late breaks (14:00+) are one-directional and day's range is mostly consumed.
✗ Don't fade a strong London trend day. The 6–10% of never-touched mid days are macro-driven, one-directional sessions. Recognize and trend with them.
✗ On high vol% IB days (>25%), tighten all targets. 92% of sessions close inside ±1× band. Extension targets are NOT valid on high-volume IB days.
✗ Don't assume London engulf means NY will engulf too. After NY takes one side in a London-engulfs-Asia pattern, only 44–45% chance the other side also gets taken.
